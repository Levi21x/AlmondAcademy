AlmondAI — The Idea
AlmondAI is an AI-powered medical education platform built specifically for Indian MBBS and NEET-PG students. The core insight is that Indian medical students are severely underserved by existing tools — they use generic AI like ChatGPT which has no understanding of Indian medical curricula, university exam patterns, or the specific pressure of NEET-PG preparation.
The core product is built around one insight: the most painful moment in a medical student's life is having an exam in 3-7 days with an entire syllabus uncovered. AlmondAI is built to rescue students in that moment — and to help students who study consistently never reach that moment in the first place.
What AlmondAI does:
The product has two modes — MBBS Companion for day-to-day college survival and NEET-PG Warrior for entrance exam preparation.
The AI Tutor is the flagship feature. It uses a RAG pipeline built on Indian medical textbooks and exam papers, combined with Groq's Llama model. The AI teaches differently based on the student's learning profile — there are 6 student categories: The Survivor (last minute studier), The Sprinter (intensive pre-exam), The Anxious Grinder (anxiety-driven consistent studier), The Passionate One (genuinely curious), The Lost One (needs foundations), and The Strategic Climber (NEET-PG rank focused). Each category gets a different teaching style, tone, and depth of explanation.
The Longitudinal Memory System is what makes AlmondAI different from simply using ChatGPT. The app tracks every question a student asks, detects patterns in what they struggle with, and uses that accumulated context to personalise every future response. After 3 months of use, the AI knows this specific student's weak points better than any generic tool ever could.
Crisis Mode is the premium emergency feature. A student inputs their exam name, date, and current preparation level. The AI analyzes their syllabus completion gaps, prioritizes topics by historical exam frequency, and generates an hour-by-hour rescue plan for the days remaining. Crucially — the app then teaches each topic in the plan right there, without the student having to go find resources elsewhere.
The Syllabus Map gives students a visual map of their entire MBBS curriculum with topic-level completion tracking. The Study Planner generates AI-powered day-by-day study plans based on exam dates and syllabus gaps. The Progress Tracker shows study streaks, weekly activity, and subject-wise completion. The MCQ Engine provides daily practice questions with detailed explanations. The Voice Agent allows fully hands-free study conversations for late night sessions.
The business model is freemium. Free users get genuine value — 15 AI questions per day, 10 MCQs, basic syllabus tracking, one Crisis Mode activation. Premium users get unlimited AI questions, unlimited Crisis Mode, advanced analytics, and the full voice agent. Pricing is designed for the Indian market: ₹199/month for MBBS, ₹399/month for NEET-PG, ₹499/month combined.
The real competitive moat is not the AI model itself — it is the data layer. Exam pattern intelligence built from systematic analysis of university-specific question papers across Indian medical universities. This institutional exam intelligence — knowing that Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences tests lipid metabolism in a specific way, or that NEET-PG has repeated the coagulation cascade 6 times in 8 years — is something no general purpose AI can replicate. This data is being built systematically through student contributions and direct university outreach.
Current build status: The product is being built by a final year B.Tech CSE-AIML student using a zero-budget local setup (RTX 3060, i9, 16GB RAM) with Next.js 14, FastAPI, Supabase, ChromaDB, LangGraph, and Groq API. Segments 1-9 of a 13-segment build plan are complete — covering auth, RAG pipeline, syllabus map, progress tracking, exam planner, longitudinal memory, voice agent, MCQ engine, and crisis mode. Remaining segments cover predictive weakness detection, visual explanations, payments, and peer intelligence.
Target market: 4-5 lakh active MBBS students in India at any given time, with a highly monetizable subset of serious NEET-PG aspirants who already spend ₹25,000-40,000/year on Marrow and PrepLadder.
